Hemoglobin Overview:
Hemoglobin is a protein in red blood cells that carries oxygen from lungs to body tissues and returns carbon dioxide to lungs.

Normal Ranges:
- Adult Males: 13.5-17.5 g/dL
- Adult Females: 12.0-15.5 g/dL
- Children: 11.0-16.0 g/dL

Low Hemoglobin (Anemia):
Causes:
- Iron deficiency
- Vitamin B12 or folate deficiency
- Chronic kidney disease
- Blood loss (menstruation, internal bleeding)
- Bone marrow disorders
- Cancer treatments

Symptoms:
- Fatigue and weakness
- Pale skin
- Shortness of breath
- Dizziness
- Cold hands and feet
- Rapid heartbeat

Treatment:
- Iron supplements (ferrous sulfate 325mg daily)
- Vitamin B12 injections or supplements
- Folate supplementation
- Dietary changes: red meat, leafy greens, legumes
- Treat underlying conditions

High Hemoglobin:
Causes:
- Dehydration
- Living at high altitudes
- Smoking
- Lung diseases (COPD)
- Heart disease
- Polycythemia vera

Recommendations:
- Stay hydrated
- Quit smoking
- Monitor oxygen levels
- Consult hematologist if persistently high

Foods to Increase Hemoglobin:
- Red meat (beef, liver)
- Poultry and fish
- Spinach, kale, broccoli
- Lentils, beans, chickpeas
- Fortified cereals
- Eggs
- Dried fruits (raisins, apricots)
- Vitamin C rich foods (citrus, tomatoes) to enhance iron absorption

When to Consult Doctor:
- Hemoglobin below 10 g/dL
- Hemoglobin above 18 g/dL
- Persistent symptoms despite treatment
- Sudden drop in levels
