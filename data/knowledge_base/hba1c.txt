HbA1c (Glycated Hemoglobin) Overview:
HbA1c measures average blood sugar levels over past 2-3 months. It's the gold standard for diabetes diagnosis and monitoring.

Normal Ranges:
- Normal: Below 5.7%
- Prediabetes: 5.7% to 6.4%
- Diabetes: 6.5% or higher
- Good diabetic control: Below 7%

Understanding HbA1c Levels:
- 5% = Average blood sugar 97 mg/dL
- 6% = Average blood sugar 126 mg/dL
- 7% = Average blood sugar 154 mg/dL
- 8% = Average blood sugar 183 mg/dL
- 9% = Average blood sugar 212 mg/dL

High HbA1c (Above 6.5%):
Risks:
- Cardiovascular disease
- Kidney damage (nephropathy)
- Nerve damage (neuropathy)
- Eye damage (retinopathy)
- Foot problems
- Infections

Causes:
- Type 1 or Type 2 diabetes
- Insulin resistance
- Poor diet high in refined carbs
- Lack of physical activity
- Stress
- Certain medications (steroids)

Lowering HbA1c:
Lifestyle Changes:
- Reduce refined carbohydrates and sugars
- Eat low glycemic index foods
- Regular exercise (150 minutes/week)
- Lose weight if overweight
- Manage stress
- Get adequate sleep (7-8 hours)

Diet Recommendations:
- Whole grains instead of refined grains
- Non-starchy vegetables
- Lean proteins
- Healthy fats (nuts, olive oil, avocado)
- Fiber-rich foods
- Limit fruit juices and sodas

Medications (If prescribed by doctor):
- Metformin
- Sulfonylureas
- GLP-1 receptor agonists
- SGLT2 inhibitors
- Insulin therapy

Monitoring:
- Check HbA1c every 3 months if diabetic
- Daily blood glucose monitoring
- Track food intake and exercise

Target Goals:
- General diabetic target: Below 7%
- Elderly or complications: Below 8%
- Young and healthy: Below 6.5%

When to Consult Doctor:
- HbA1c above 6.5% (first diagnosis)
- HbA1c above 9% (urgent)
- Symptoms: excessive thirst, frequent urination, blurred vision
- Cannot reach target despite lifestyle changes
