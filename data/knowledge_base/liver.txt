Liver Function Tests (LFTs) Overview:
Liver processes nutrients, filters toxins, and produces essential proteins. LFTs assess liver health.

Key Tests:
1. ALT (Alanine Aminotransferase) - Enzyme mainly in liver
2. AST (Aspartate Aminotransferase) - Enzyme in liver and other organs
3. ALP (Alkaline Phosphatase) - Enzyme in liver and bones
4. Bilirubin - Breakdown product of red blood cells
5. Albumin - Protein made by liver

Normal Ranges:
ALT (SGPT):
- Normal: 7-56 U/L

AST (SGOT):
- Normal: 10-40 U/L

ALP:
- Normal: 44-147 U/L

Bilirubin:
- Total: 0.1-1.2 mg/dL
- Direct: 0.0-0.3 mg/dL

Albumin:
- Normal: 3.5-5.5 g/dL

Elevated Liver Enzymes Causes:
- Fatty liver disease (NAFLD)
- Hepatitis (A, B, C)
- Alcohol-related liver disease
- Medications (acetaminophen, statins)
- Obesity
- Diabetes
- Cirrhosis
- Liver cancer
- Autoimmune hepatitis

Symptoms of Liver Problems:
- Jaundice (yellow skin/eyes)
- Abdominal pain (upper right)
- Swelling in legs and abdomen
- Dark urine
- Pale stools
- Chronic fatigue
- Nausea and vomiting
- Loss of appetite
- Easy bruising

Protecting Liver Health:

Diet Recommendations:
- Mediterranean diet
- High fiber foods
- Lean proteins
- Healthy fats
- Limit saturated fats
- Reduce sugar intake
- Avoid alcohol or limit significantly

Foods Good for Liver:
- Coffee (2-3 cups daily)
- Green tea
- Fatty fish (salmon, sardines)
- Nuts (walnuts, almonds)
- Olive oil
- Berries
- Cruciferous vegetables (broccoli, Brussels sprouts)
- Beets
- Citrus fruits
- Grapes

Foods to Avoid:
- Alcohol
- Fried foods
- High-fat meats
- Processed foods
- Excess sugar
- White bread and pasta
- Excess salt

Lifestyle Changes:
- Maintain healthy weight
- Exercise regularly (150 min/week)
- Avoid toxins and chemicals
- Careful with medications
- Get vaccinated (Hepatitis A & B)
- Practice safe behaviors

Supplements (Consult doctor first):
- Vitamin E (for NAFLD)
- Milk thistle
- Omega-3 fatty acids
- Vitamin D

Medications to Be Cautious With:
- Acetaminophen (Tylenol) - max 3000mg/day
- NSAIDs
- Certain antibiotics
- Statins (monitor LFTs)
- Herbal supplements

Weight Loss for Fatty Liver:
- Lose 7-10% of body weight
- Reduce calories by 500-1000/day
- Combine diet and exercise
- Avoid crash diets

When to Consult Doctor:
- ALT or AST above 100 U/L
- Persistent elevation for 3+ months
- Symptoms of liver disease
- Jaundice
- Abdominal swelling
- ALT/AST ratio concerning

When to See Hepatologist:
- Chronic hepatitis
- Cirrhosis
- Liver enzyme levels >3x normal
- Suspected liver disease
- Abnormal bilirubin or albumin

Emergency Signs:
- Severe abdominal pain
- Confusion or altered mental state
- Vomiting blood
- Black stools
- Severe jaundice
