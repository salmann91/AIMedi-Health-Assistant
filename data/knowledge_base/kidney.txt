Kidney Function Tests Overview:
Kidneys filter waste and excess fluid from blood. Tests measure how well kidneys are working.

Key Tests:
1. Creatinine - Waste product from muscle metabolism
2. BUN (Blood Urea Nitrogen) - Measures urea nitrogen
3. eGFR (Estimated Glomerular Filtration Rate) - Kidney filtration rate

Normal Ranges:
Creatinine:
- Adult Males: 0.7-1.3 mg/dL
- Adult Females: 0.6-1.1 mg/dL

BUN:
- Normal: 7-20 mg/dL

eGFR:
- Normal: Above 90 mL/min/1.73m²
- Stage 1 CKD: 90+ (with kidney damage)
- Stage 2 CKD: 60-89
- Stage 3 CKD: 30-59
- Stage 4 CKD: 15-29
- Stage 5 CKD: Below 15 (kidney failure)

High Creatinine Causes:
- Chronic kidney disease
- Acute kidney injury
- Dehydration
- High protein diet
- Intense exercise
- Certain medications (NSAIDs, ACE inhibitors)
- Urinary tract obstruction
- Muscle disorders

Symptoms of Kidney Problems:
- Fatigue and weakness
- Swelling in legs, ankles, feet
- Decreased urine output
- Shortness of breath
- Confusion
- Nausea and vomiting
- Chest pain
- High blood pressure

Protecting Kidney Health:

Diet Recommendations:
- Limit sodium (below 2300mg/day)
- Reduce protein if advanced CKD
- Limit potassium if levels high
- Control phosphorus intake
- Stay hydrated (unless fluid restricted)
- Avoid processed foods

Foods Good for Kidneys:
- Cabbage, cauliflower
- Berries (blueberries, strawberries)
- Apples
- Fish (omega-3)
- Egg whites
- Olive oil
- Garlic and onions

Foods to Limit:
- Red meat
- Dairy products
- Processed foods
- Bananas, oranges (high potassium)
- Tomatoes
- Dark colas
- Nuts and seeds (if potassium high)

Lifestyle Changes:
- Control blood pressure (target: below 130/80)
- Manage diabetes (HbA1c below 7%)
- Exercise regularly
- Maintain healthy weight
- Quit smoking
- Limit alcohol
- Avoid NSAIDs (ibuprofen, naproxen)

Medications to Discuss with Doctor:
- ACE inhibitors or ARBs for blood pressure
- Diuretics for fluid retention
- Phosphate binders
- Vitamin D supplements
- Erythropoietin for anemia

Monitoring:
- Check creatinine and eGFR every 3-6 months
- Monitor blood pressure daily
- Urine tests for protein
- Electrolyte levels

When to Consult Nephrologist:
- Creatinine above 2.0 mg/dL
- eGFR below 60
- Protein in urine
- Rapid decline in kidney function
- Difficult to control blood pressure
- Stage 4 or 5 CKD

Emergency Signs:
- Severely decreased or no urine output
- Extreme swelling
- Confusion or altered mental state
- Chest pain
- Severe shortness of breath
