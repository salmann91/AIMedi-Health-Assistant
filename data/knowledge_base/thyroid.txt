Thyroid Function Tests Overview:
Thyroid gland regulates metabolism, energy, and body temperature through hormones.

Key Tests:
1. TSH (Thyroid Stimulating Hormone) - Pituitary hormone controlling thyroid
2. T4 (Thyroxine) - Main thyroid hormone
3. T3 (Triiodothyronine) - Active thyroid hormone
4. Free T4 and Free T3 - Unbound, active forms

Normal Ranges:
TSH:
- Normal: 0.4-4.0 μIU/mL
- Optimal: 1.0-2.0 μIU/mL

T4 (Total):
- Normal: 5.0-12.0 μg/dL

Free T4:
- Normal: 0.8-1.8 ng/dL

T3 (Total):
- Normal: 80-200 ng/dL

Free T3:
- Normal: 2.3-4.2 pg/mL

Hypothyroidism (Underactive Thyroid):
TSH High, T4 Low

Causes:
- Hashimoto's thyroiditis (autoimmune)
- Iodine deficiency
- Thyroid surgery or radiation
- Medications (lithium, amiodarone)
- Pituitary disorders
- Congenital hypothyroidism

Symptoms:
- Fatigue and weakness
- Weight gain
- Cold intolerance
- Dry skin and hair
- Hair loss
- Constipation
- Depression
- Brain fog
- Slow heart rate
- Heavy menstrual periods

Treatment:
- Levothyroxine (Synthroid) - taken on empty stomach
- Monitor TSH every 6-8 weeks initially
- Lifelong treatment usually required
- Take medication consistently

Hyperthyroidism (Overactive Thyroid):
TSH Low, T4/T3 High

Causes:
- Graves' disease (autoimmune)
- Toxic nodular goiter
- Thyroiditis
- Excess iodine
- Too much thyroid medication

Symptoms:
- Weight loss despite increased appetite
- Rapid heartbeat
- Anxiety and irritability
- Tremors
- Sweating
- Heat intolerance
- Frequent bowel movements
- Sleep problems
- Bulging eyes (Graves')

Treatment:
- Antithyroid medications (Methimazole)
- Radioactive iodine therapy
- Beta blockers for symptoms
- Surgery in some cases

Diet for Thyroid Health:

Foods to Eat:
- Iodized salt (moderate amounts)
- Seafood and fish
- Eggs
- Dairy products
- Selenium-rich foods (Brazil nuts, tuna)
- Zinc-rich foods (beef, shellfish)
- Iron-rich foods
- Fruits and vegetables

For Hypothyroidism:
- Adequate iodine
- Selenium supplements
- Vitamin D
- Avoid excessive soy
- Limit cruciferous vegetables if raw

For Hyperthyroidism:
- Low iodine diet if preparing for treatment
- Avoid iodine supplements
- Cruciferous vegetables (may be helpful)
- Increase calories during active phase

Lifestyle Recommendations:
- Manage stress (affects thyroid)
- Get adequate sleep
- Regular exercise
- Avoid smoking
- Limit alcohol
- Maintain healthy weight

Medications Affecting Thyroid:
- Take levothyroxine alone, 30-60 min before food
- Separate from calcium, iron supplements (4 hours)
- Separate from coffee (30-60 min)
- Biotin can interfere with tests

Monitoring:
- TSH test every 6-12 months once stable
- Adjust medication based on symptoms and levels
- Check thyroid antibodies if autoimmune suspected
- Ultrasound if nodules present

When to Consult Endocrinologist:
- TSH above 10 or below 0.1
- Difficulty controlling thyroid levels
- Pregnancy with thyroid disorder
- Thyroid nodules or goiter
- Suspected thyroid cancer
- Unusual symptoms

Thyroid and Pregnancy:
- Increased medication needs
- Check TSH every 4 weeks
- Target TSH: 0.1-2.5 (first trimester)
- Critical for fetal development

Emergency Signs:
- Thyroid storm (severe hyperthyroidism):
  * Fever above 103°F
  * Rapid heart rate >140
  * Confusion, agitation
  * Requires immediate hospitalization
  
- Myxedema coma (severe hypothyroidism):
  * Extreme fatigue
  * Confusion
  * Low body temperature
  * Requires emergency treatment
