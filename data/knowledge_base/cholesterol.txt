Cholesterol Overview:
Cholesterol is a waxy substance used to build cells and produce hormones. Too much cholesterol increases heart disease risk.

Types of Cholesterol:
1. LDL (Low-Density Lipoprotein) - "Bad" cholesterol
2. HDL (High-Density Lipoprotein) - "Good" cholesterol
3. Triglycerides - Fat in blood
4. Total Cholesterol - Sum of all cholesterol types

Normal Ranges:
Total Cholesterol:
- Desirable: Below 200 mg/dL
- Borderline high: 200-239 mg/dL
- High: 240 mg/dL and above

LDL Cholesterol:
- Optimal: Below 100 mg/dL
- Near optimal: 100-129 mg/dL
- Borderline high: 130-159 mg/dL
- High: 160-189 mg/dL
- Very high: 190 mg/dL and above

HDL Cholesterol:
- Poor: Below 40 mg/dL (men), Below 50 mg/dL (women)
- Good: 60 mg/dL and above

Triglycerides:
- Normal: Below 150 mg/dL
- Borderline high: 150-199 mg/dL
- High: 200-499 mg/dL
- Very high: 500 mg/dL and above

High Cholesterol Risks:
- Heart attack
- Stroke
- Peripheral artery disease
- Atherosclerosis (plaque buildup in arteries)
- Angina (chest pain)

Causes of High Cholesterol:
- Unhealthy diet (saturated fats, trans fats)
- Obesity
- Lack of exercise
- Smoking
- Diabetes
- Genetics (familial hypercholesterolemia)
- Age (risk increases with age)

Lowering Cholesterol:

Dietary Changes:
- Reduce saturated fats (red meat, butter, cheese)
- Eliminate trans fats (fried foods, packaged snacks)
- Eat omega-3 fatty acids (salmon, mackerel, walnuts)
- Increase soluble fiber (oats, beans, apples)
- Add whey protein
- Plant sterols and stanols

Foods to Eat:
- Oatmeal and high-fiber cereals
- Fatty fish (salmon, sardines)
- Nuts (almonds, walnuts)
- Olive oil
- Avocados
- Beans and legumes
- Vegetables and fruits
- Whole grains

Foods to Avoid:
- Red meat
- Full-fat dairy
- Fried foods
- Processed meats
- Baked goods with trans fats
- Sugary foods

Lifestyle Changes:
- Exercise 30 minutes daily
- Lose excess weight
- Quit smoking
- Limit alcohol
- Manage stress

Medications (If prescribed):
- Statins (Atorvastatin, Rosuvastatin)
- Ezetimibe
- PCSK9 inhibitors
- Bile acid sequestrants
- Fibrates (for high triglycerides)

When to Consult Doctor:
- LDL above 190 mg/dL
- Total cholesterol above 240 mg/dL
- Family history of early heart disease
- HDL below 40 mg/dL
- Triglycerides above 500 mg/dL
